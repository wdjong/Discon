DISCON

Play

Move by dragging your number piece in a L-Shape, 2 squares in 1 direction then 1 square at right angles, like a chess knight

When you land on a coloured piece it becomes part of your tower. When you drag it to your next move it remains part of your tower.

If you want to leave a tower behind, you should use the right button to drag your numbered piece.

You can click on a tower to see its constituent colours.

Other version ideas (from original rules)

Elementary game (for children)

Try to collect a disc of any colour with every move. The player who first erects the required number of towers of maximum height wins.

Requirements:

  • 2 players: 4 towers, 12 storeys high.
  • 3 players: 4 towers, 8 storeys high.
  • 4 players: 3 towers, 8 storeys high.

For small children reduce height and increase number of towers.

Basic game

Play continues until all discs are collected. Each player may use all colours at random to erect any number of towers up to 12 storeys high. Score 2 points for each violet disc, 4 points for a blue or a pink disc, and 8 points for each ivory disc.

Variation 1.

Each tower use be composed of 2 colours, or one colour only.

Variation 2.

Each tower must be of single colour only.

Advanced premium game

Premium game with additional premium for erecting towers with 2 colours alternating (i.e. pink-blue-pink-blue etc.). For such towers allocate 3 times the mixed colour score for appropriate height. (This is not implemented in this version)

Alternating colours: Allocate three times the mixed colour score for appropriate height.

Penalty: For each excess storey reduce the maximum allowed height by one, and score accordingly. (I.e. if max. height = 8; a 9-storey tower scores 7-storeys; a 10-storey tower scores 6 storeys etc.)

Further versions

The players can easily invent other versions. They may specify different and/or more colour-combinations, change the height regulations, and alter the scoring system. They can even modify the method of play. (This implementation doesn't support any of this)')

A competition game may be organized by setting up two or more boards with identical pattern and appropriately increasing the number of players.
